零空间历史
LAB ZERO story
Makerspace is community of makers and hackers that are interested in physical computing, Internet of Things, and generally building things
2013 ~ TechSpace
Shang Song (尚松) opened the place as one of the first makerspaces in Shenzhen. It gathered members from many different countries.

TechSpace was focused on providing startups access to factories and the local supply chain. Since the main focus was assisting startups and not promoting the makerspace, in May 2015 it was closed.
2015 ~ Litchee Lab
After Techspace was closed, Lit Liao (廖丽婷) and other members decided to start Litchee Lab in the same place, some equipment was also moved from TechSpace.

A distinctive feature of this new space was using 1/2 of the space for makerspace and the other half for kids education business, which was also owned by Lit. In August 2017 Lit decided to move her kids education business to a new location.

Members are now faced with the new challenge of finding the money to run the space independently.
2017 ~ LAB ZERO
After Lit's company moved out, current members decided to rename the makerspace to LAB ZERO and start the next chapter of this story, with a new vision of making it a world-class makerspace.
